## 💼 LINKEDIN CUT
**Hook line:** I stopped doing the consulting work. I built the machine that builds the machine — and it gets smarter every engagement.

**Body:**
Most consultants sell hours. I got tired of standing up the same AI operating system by hand for every company, so I built a meta-factory: a "council" that scaffolds a purpose-built factory for each engagement.

Here's how it actually runs. I describe a mission — the north star, who signs off, who executes. The council captures that brief, then renders a working factory: its own operating brain, its own agents, its own stage gates, its own work tracker. One pass. It's all Claude Code, and a folder of markdown files.

The receipts are in one file — `council/runs.jsonl`. Two real missions live there today: a Microsoft-enterprise pilot factory (regulytics) and a curriculum engine that mass-produces sellable build-guides (stepahead). Each row logs what got emitted and — the part that matters — a `template_debt` field: everything the build noticed was over- or under-built.

That field is the whole point. At review, recurring debt becomes a rule. When the curriculum engine taught me the product was a 1-page prompt-pack, not a 23-page document, that correction got encoded into the template — so the next factory inherits it. Lessons become edits, not tribal memory. Every factory ships leaner than the last.

The guardrail is the flex: the AI proposes the lesson, I dispose. Nothing gets templated until it's earned its place across two real builds.

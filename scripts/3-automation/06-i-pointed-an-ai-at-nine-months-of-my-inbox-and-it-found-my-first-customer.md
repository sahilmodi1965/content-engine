## 💼 LINKEDIN CUT
**Hook line:** I pointed an AI at nine months of my inbox. It handed me my first-ever paying customer — someone I'd completely forgotten.
**Body:**
Your inbox is the most honest CRM you own and the one you'll never re-read. Every real relationship you've built is in there, decaying, because you don't have time to mine nine months of email by hand.

So I had my system do it. It read about nine months of Gmail and reconstructed roughly fifty candidate relationships, grouped into six clusters. Two of them stopped me: my first-ever paying customer, and a director of partnerships whose role I'd half-forgotten I even had a line to.

The part that makes it safe to run on a real business is the discipline. Nothing gets written until I approve it, one row at a time. Every contact it proposes carries the source thread it came from and a confidence score, so I can verify the claim before it enters my system. The AI proposes. I dispose.

This is what leverage looks like applied to relationships: not more outreach, but remembering everything you already earned.

**CTA:** Your best leads are buried in your inbox right now. DM me "AUTOMATE" and I'll build the thing that digs them out — without writing a word you didn't approve.

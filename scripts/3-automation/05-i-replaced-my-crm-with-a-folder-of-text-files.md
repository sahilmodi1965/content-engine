**Body:**
Every CRM I've ever paid for died the same way: nobody updates it, so it slowly fills with stale fields and stops telling the truth. The tool became the work.

So I replaced it with a folder. Plain text files — 81 contacts, 32 organizations, 16 relationship types, four live deals, each running as its own pipeline. No Salesforce, no Airtable, no monthly bill.

I run it by talking to it. I type "morning" and get a ranked digest of who I should reach today. It preps me for a meeting twenty-four hours ahead without being asked. And my confidence in a relationship auto-decays over time, so a contact I've gone quiet on surfaces back up before it goes cold.

The point isn't that text files are clever. It's that the system gives me answers instead of demanding data entry. A CRM should tell you who to call. Mine does, in a folder I own forever.

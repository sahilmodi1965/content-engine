## 💼 LINKEDIN CUT
**Hook line:** I let an AI read nine months of my own inbox. It found a paying customer I had genuinely forgotten existed.

**Body:**
No CRM. No Airtable. No data-entry intern. I pointed an agent at the raw email and gave it one plain-English instruction: reconstruct my business.

It came back with a manifest — every relationship clustered by thread. And in those clusters were two things I'd lost: a customer who had actually paid me, and a work email tied to a job I'd half-remembered. Nine months of context I was carrying in my head, and carrying badly. The software wasn't.

Here's the part that matters more than the find. The agent can read everything and decide nothing. Every discovery lands in an approve / skip / defer queue, and I clear it by hand. It can see my entire inbox and structurally cannot act on it. The AI proposes. I dispose.

That's the whole trick to trusting one of these with your real business: read-only by default, human at the gate. The leverage isn't that it works while I sleep — it's that it reconstructs what I forgot, and still asks permission before it moves.

I'm building this one in public, cluster by cluster.

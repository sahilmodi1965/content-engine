## 💼 LINKEDIN CUT
**Hook line:** My classroom whiteboard is a config file. I recompile it by describing the lesson out loud.

**Body:**
Most people teaching "how to build with AI" stand in front of a slide deck. That always felt like a lie to me — you can't teach building from a tool that can't build.

So my teaching board isn't a board. It's a config file. I describe the lesson out loud, run `node generate.js`, and a 4K board renders to the 65-inch TV on my wall. Change one value in the file, the whole board updates, nothing drifts. No Canva, no PowerPoint, no fiddling with text boxes at 2am.

The point isn't the board. The point is that the way I teach IS the thing I'm teaching: one source of truth, one command, the artifact is the product. Students don't watch me talk about leverage — they watch the board recompile while I'm mid-sentence.

That's why I run it live. Every two weeks I open the factory and build something from scratch while you watch the whole terminal — including the teaching tool itself.

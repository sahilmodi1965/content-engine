# voice-profile.md — Sahil Modi

> The persona: a solo founder who runs an agentic empire from a terminal. No team, no SaaS subscriptions, no agency he fully trusts. Just Claude Code, a folder of markdown files, and a command word. He's not selling software — he's selling *the way he works*, and the implicit dare: "everyone needs to be on this."

## Who he is on camera

- **Builder, not guru.** He shows the terminal, the repo, the live URL. The proof IS the content. He never asks you to take his word — he scrolls the receipts.
- **Solo operator with leverage envy as the hook.** The recurring flex is not "I work hard" — it's "I built the thing that does the work." *"I'm not building a game, I built the thing that builds games."* *"A factory that builds factories."* He sells the exoskeleton, not the muscle.
- **Founder-to-founder, slightly relentless.** He talks to other operators like a peer who's three moves ahead and a little impatient that they're still doing it by hand. Warm but pointed: *"You're feeding the ad optimizer garbage and you don't even know it."*
- **Proof-over-hype, allergic to drift.** His whole architecture is anti-bullshit: cold-run gates, faithfulness gates, "it's live is said only after the push succeeds," dedup by shared event_id. He carries that into his content — every claim has a file path behind it.

## How he actually writes (study cues from his repos)

- **Terse. Table-first. Numbers before narrative.** (FB Agent: "Terse. Table-first. Numbers before narrative.") Lead with the metric, then the story.
- **The artifact is the product.** "The HTML is the product. You write it like a designer, not a programmer." He frames tools as living things with a single source of truth.
- **Command-word energy.** He types one word — `go`, `refresh`, `morning`, `prep me for 4pm` — and a system moves. Dramatize the single command vs. the giant outcome.
- **Rules mined from real failure, each with a name attached.** "Balkrishna ghosted after 5 messages." "I stopped telling Sonal her work was published." His credibility comes from naming the exact mistake the rule was born from.
- **Guardrail-first integrity.** "Read-only by default." "The credential never enters Claude's context." "The AI proposes, Sahil disposes." This is a *trust* signal — lean into it, it's a differentiator.
- **Plain English to the machine.** He talks to his systems like people: "who haven't I talked to in 30 days that I should." Show the conversational input, then the polished output.

## Energy & pacing

- Open cold with the impossible-sounding claim or the live screen. No "hey guys."
- Mid-clip: one moment of *receipt* — scroll the runs.jsonl, the metrics table, the green PR checks, the kid grinning at their 3D world.
- Close with a dare or an invitation, not a beg. He's letting you in on something, not pitching.
- He's allowed to be a little smug — but it's *earned* smug, always backed by a file path.

## Do

- Show the terminal and the live result in the SAME clip. Input → output.
- Put the number on screen as text (1,171 installs, 81 contacts, 159 runs, 142 slides).
- Name the real human in the story (Shahriar, Kimaya, Sonal, the agency, his first customer).
- Frame the guardrail as a flex ("it can see my ad money but structurally can't spend it").
- Make the viewer feel behind — kindly. "You're still doing this by hand."

## Don't

- Don't explain the tech stack unless the stack IS the punchline. Outcome first.
- Don't hedge or over-qualify. He ships drafts; he doesn't ask permission in public.
- Don't use generic hustle-bro language ("grind," "10x your life"). His flex is *leverage*, not effort.
- Don't claim what isn't in a file. If there's no receipt, it's not a Sahil story.
- No emojis in his speaking voice (his reports use 🚩 functionally, but he talks plainly).
